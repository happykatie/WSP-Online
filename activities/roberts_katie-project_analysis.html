Site Intent

The website re-design goals of the City of Okeechobee are to reorganize and condense the current content, and refocus the content to the interests of the residents of Okeechobee. Condensing the website navigation down to 6 menu items, instead of the current 15, will simplify the user experience by helping them locate what what they are looking for faster. The overall look of the site is confusing. There are excess links, including external links in the navigation menu that need to be reorganized under one menu item. The content is heavily focused towards the interests of the government officials, instead of addressing the interests of the community. To reach the target audience and increase web traffic, most of the current content can be placed under a "Government" section, making room to focus on the members of the community and community events. The visual flow can be improved by adjusting the layout to include web-optimized images and visual hierarchy to the text.

By organizing and reducing the current content, as well as adding content that engages the community, the navigation can be reduced to the following 6 pages:

  • Home
  • Events
  • Community
  • Government
  • Departments
  • Contacts

To further suit the needs of the community and increase web traffic, links to register for online utility billing and to pay bills, such as water and trash collection, should be present on the home page for easy access. By adding a bill pay option that would likely be utilized monthly, and shifting to a community focus, the site traffic will increase.
